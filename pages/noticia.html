Max Holloway beats Conor McGregor in 69 seconds at UFC 329

The Hawaiian defeated the Irishman by technical knockout in the very first round, after McGregor suffered a knee injury attempting a jumping kick at the start of the bout, in his first return to the octagon since 2021.

RN
Rafael Nogueira
12 July 2026 • Updated 14 July 2026 • 5 min read

T-Mobile Arena, Las Vegas — venue of UFC 329. Illustrative image.

UFC 329, held on 11 July at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas, ended abruptly: Max Holloway defeated Conor McGregor by technical knockout after just 1 minute and 9 seconds of action. The result came after McGregor suffered a knee injury in the opening exchange, attempting a jumping kick at the start of the bout — the referee stopped the fight seconds later, confirming Holloway's win.

The rematch marked McGregor's return to the octagon after a gap since 2021, a period marked by injuries and disputes outside the cage. Anticipation around the fight was high, given the history between the two fighters and the length of the Irishman's absence from the organisation.

In the days following the event, McGregor commented on social media about the result, expressing frustration with how the fight ended. World MMA did not have direct access to the original post and, for that reason, chose not to reproduce specific excerpts pending confirmation from a primary source.

A card packed with submissions and knockouts

Beyond the main event, the UFC 329 card delivered an intense run of quick finishes. Paddy Pimblett submitted Benoît Saint Denis with a D'Arce choke in just 52 seconds of the first round. In the bantamweight division, Mario Bautista defeated Cory Sandhagen by unanimous decision.

Other highlights of the night included Brandon Royval's submission win over Lone'er Kavanagh in the third round, King Green's first-round technical knockout of Terrance McKinney, and technical knockout victories for Robert Whittaker over Nikita Krylov and Gable Steveson over Elisha Ellison.

What's next

With the win, Max Holloway cements his position as one of the UFC's most consistent names, while Conor McGregor's immediate future in the octagon remains uncertain following his new injury. The organisation continues its run of events in the coming weeks, including UFC Fight Night Oklahoma City (18 July) and UFC Fight Night Abu Dhabi (25 July), plus the numbered event UFC 330, set for 15 August, featuring a welterweight title fight between Islam Makhachev and Ian Machado Garry.
