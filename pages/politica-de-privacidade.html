1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how World MMA ("we", "us", "the portal") collects, uses, stores and protects the information of website visitors, in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and other applicable legislation.

By using our website, you agree to the practices described in this document. We recommend reading it in full before browsing or submitting any personal data.

2. Data we collect

We may collect the following categories of data:

  • Data you provide voluntarily: name and email address, when you subscribe to the newsletter or fill in the contact form;
  • Browsing data: IP address, device type, browser, pages visited, time on site and referral source;
  • Advertising campaign data: click identifiers and UTM parameters used to measure the source of traffic from ads (e.g. Snapchat Ads, Taboola, Pinterest Ads).

3. How we use your data

We use the data we collect to:

  • Send the newsletter and communications you have requested;
  • Reply to messages sent through the contact form;
  • Improve the browsing experience and site performance;
  • Measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and editorial content;
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

4. Cookies and tracking technologies

We use first-party and third-party cookies to enable site functionality and measure audience. Full details on the types of cookies used, their purposes and how to manage them are set out in our Cookie Policy.

5. Sharing with third parties

We may use third-party tools for audience analysis and advertising measurement, including but not limited to: Google Analytics 4, Google Tag Manager, Meta Pixel, Pinterest Tag, Snap Pixel and Microsoft Clarity. These tools may collect browsing data independently, in accordance with each provider's own privacy policy.

We do not sell personal data to third parties. Sharing only takes place to fulfil the purposes described in this policy.

6. Legal basis for processing

World MMA processes personal data on the basis of the data subject's consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR), our legitimate interest in improving the service (Article 6(1)(f)), and, where applicable, compliance with a legal obligation.

7. Data retention

We keep personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law. Newsletter data is retained until the data subject requests to unsubscribe.

8. Information security

We adopt reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, alteration or improper disclosure. Despite our efforts, no system is entirely free of risk, and we recommend caution when sharing sensitive information online.

9. Your rights (UK GDPR)

As a data subject, you have the right to:

  • Be informed about how your data is processed;
  • Access your personal data;
  • Have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected;
  • Request erasure or restriction of processing of unnecessary data;
  • Withdraw your consent at any time;
  • Request data portability;
  • Object to processing, including for direct marketing purposes;
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk if you believe your rights have not been respected.

To exercise these rights, contact us at world.mma001@gmail.com.

10. Children

Our website is not directed at anyone under 18 and we do not knowingly collect data from children without appropriate parental or guardian consent, as required by applicable data protection law.

11. Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically. The date of the last update will always appear at the top of this page. We recommend reviewing it from time to time.

12. Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to world.mma001@gmail.com or via our contact page.
